CAUSES OF PAKISTAN

No Proper Education

Education is a major cause of concern in Pakistan, with many children not having access to quality education. This is due to a lack of schools, trained teachers, and resources in many areas of the country. According to a report by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), the literacy rate in Pakistan is only 58%, with a significant gap between the literacy rates of men and women.

Water Crisis

Water scarcity is a major problem in Pakistan, and it is becoming increasingly severe as the population continues to grow. The country's water resources are under tremendous pressure due to a combination of factors such as over-extraction of groundwater, lack of proper water management, and inadequate infrastructure.

Poor Roads

The poor condition of roads in Pakistan is a major issue that contributes to a high number of accidents in the country. The road network in Pakistan is inadequate, poorly maintained, and lacks proper infrastructure, such as well-marked lanes, traffic signals, and median barriers. This makes the roads unsafe for both drivers and pedestrians.

Electricity is a major issue in Pakistan, and it is characterized by a lack of supply and high bills.
